Chicago Flying Tips

For Private Pilots

MIDWAY

Midway International Airport (KMDW) is the sister airport of Chicago O'Hare International Airport and serves as Southwest Airlines' Chicago hub. The airport is home to several Fixed Base Operators (FBOs) for general aviation, including Atlantic and Signature. There are a couple of flight schools for those interested in training at a busy airport. This is where I did my training!

  • Midway Aeronautical Information

GARY

Gary/Chicago International Airport (KGYY) is an airport near the border of Indiana and Illinois. You will usually see private jet traffic here, but can occasionally find military airplanes. During the Chicago Air and Water Show, the fighter jets and stunt planes land and take off here. There are a few FBOs here, including Gary Jet Center. I flew out of KGYY on many solo cross-country flights!

  • Visit Gary/Chicago's Website
  • Gary/Chicago Aeronautical Information

LANSING

Lansing Municipal Airport (KIGQ) is a non-towered airport near the border of Indiana and Illinois. The airport is home to a few small FBOs and a helicopter flight school. You will tend to find student pilot traffic here, and it may get fairly busy on a nice day. There are four runways; north, south, east, and west, ensuring more manangable crosswinds for landing. This is where I did my first solo flight!
